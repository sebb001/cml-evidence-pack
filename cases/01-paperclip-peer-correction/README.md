## Governance situation

A strong initial analysis was treated as provisional, not authoritative. The assessment was peer-reviewed, corrected on specific factual points, and revised without hiding the original record.

The governance situation is familiar: a thorough technical assessment looks persuasive, but another capable actor finds material flaws. In many organisations, that becomes either an argument, a quiet rewrite, or a loss of confidence in the process. Here the correction gets a formal lane. The original work remains visible, the challenge is attached to the relevant claim, and the revised position becomes inspectable.

## Judgment evidenced

The evidence shows disagreement becoming useful rather than political. Mistakes do not disappear; they are found, owned, and folded into the decision record without forcing a choice between authority and truth.
