The [case JSON](case.json) is authoritative; this brief is navigation.

## Situation

A strong initial analysis was treated as provisional, not authoritative. The assessment was peer-reviewed, corrected on specific factual points, and revised without hiding the original record.

The governance situation is familiar: a thorough technical assessment looks persuasive, but another capable actor finds material flaws. In many organisations, that becomes either an argument, a quiet rewrite, or a loss of confidence in the process. Here the correction gets a formal lane. The original work remains visible, the challenge is attached to the relevant claim, and the revised position becomes inspectable.

The system records the original interpretation, the review request, the reviewer correction, the author response, and the revised operative interpretation as distinct custody points.

## Why it matters

This is judgment under correction pressure. Disagreement becomes useful rather than political: mistakes do not disappear; they are found, owned, and folded into the decision record without forcing a choice between authority and truth. The record shows a preference for traceable revision over defensiveness: keep the first analysis, accept the specific correction, and make the revised view the working basis.
